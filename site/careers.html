Data Analyst
Position Summary
511 Operator plays a pivotal role in collecting and disseminating traffic and transit emergency and non-emergency information assisting in congestion management in the San Francisco Bay Area along with Santa Cruz and San Joaquin county. The timeliness and quality of the information is a key in this role as thousands of travelers depend on this information. The operator shall utilize software (Traffic and Transit Reporting and Management System (TRAMS)) to disseminate information on multiple dissemination channels inclusive of web portal, phone, social media and dynamic message signs.
This opportunity would be a great fit for someone who is data/quality centric and thrives in a fast-paced environment. Position works on a shift basis, and the hours may vary; and staff availability during emergencies is an important consideration for this position.
Responsibilities
The TIC Operators are responsible for collecting, monitoring and posting of the traffic and transit information. The traffic incident information from California Highway Patrol CAD and Lane Closure System automatically gets logged into the Traffic and Transit Reporting and Management System (TRAMS), the Operator is responsible to ensure the incident details are readable and can be understood by the travelers. Transit Incident Information is received from a variety of media including e-mails, tweets and other sources, and is entered into the system for dissemination on both the phone and web. Other duties include detecting scheduled and unscheduled traffic/transit and incident management events, congestion and travel time imbalances in the geographical coverage area with monitoring and/or surveillance systems.
Job Requirements
  • Obtain and post incident status information to and from multiple agencies
  • Monitor traffic/transit status, construction information, scheduled events and active events
  • Produce daily shift, equipment malfunction, activity and phone logs
  • Periodic system checks of the dissemination channels and the information being disseminated
  • Support during the emergencies
  • Participate in the regular operations training
